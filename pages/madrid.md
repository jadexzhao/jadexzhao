# madrid

*spring 2026. universidad complutense.*

---

i came here to study how culture shapes the way people adopt technology.

i didn't expect the city to study me back.

---

madrid moves differently. not slower, exactly. just less apologetic about existing. people sit in cafés for two hours and don't feel like they're wasting time. they eat late and mean it. the light in the evening does something i don't have words for yet.

i spent the first few weeks feeling like i was behind. like i should be producing something, proving something, justifying the grant and the plane ticket and the absence.

somewhere around week four i stopped doing that.

---

what i was actually researching: how cultural context shapes technology adoption. startups next to century-old institutions. fintech in a country that still closes for lunch. digital transformation happening inside systems that were never designed for it.

what i was actually learning: that i already knew more than i thought. that being 4,000 miles from everything familiar is clarifying in a way that comfort never is. that first-gen doesn't mean behind. it means you built the map yourself.

---

the café con leche every morning didn't hurt.

---

i'm back now. same girl from indianapolis. but something got rearranged.

i think that's the point.
