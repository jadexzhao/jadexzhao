# the pb sandwich lesson

a professor asks students to write instructions for making a peanut butter sandwich.

the class writes things like "spread peanut butter on the bread."

the professor follows exactly what they wrote. opens nothing. assumes nothing. spreads nothing.

he places the sealed jar on top of the uncut loaf and waits.

---

computers are that professor.

so is every system you build. every prompt you write. every handoff you make. every form a survivor fills out at 2am trying to find shelter. every civic portal a resident navigates to report a broken streetlight. every health dashboard a researcher uses to track disease patterns across a county.

if you didn't say it, it didn't happen.
if you assumed it, you lost someone.

---

this is why i care about informatics the way i do.

not because i love technology. but because i've seen what happens when the people building systems forget that the person on the other end isn't them.

accessibility isn't a checklist. it's the difference between a door that opens and a door that doesn't.

clarity isn't a style preference. it's the difference between a form someone can complete and one they abandon.

---

everything i build starts here.

ratio. logos. ordo. praxis.

think carefully. reason clearly. order well. then act.

the sandwich doesn't make itself.
