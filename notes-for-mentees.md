# notes for mentees

things i wish someone had said to me earlier. some of them i had to learn sideways.

---

**you're not behind.**

whatever timeline you're measuring yourself against, it's probably someone else's. first-gen students especially — we enter rooms that weren't built for us and then wonder why the doors feel heavy. that's not a you problem.

---

**the pb sandwich rule.**

be precise. in your code, your writing, your asks, your communication. don't assume the person on the other end knows what you meant. they are following your instructions exactly as written. be generous with clarity.

---

**serve something real.**

the best technical work i've done has been for people who actually needed it. a nonprofit website redesign. a city accessibility audit. a civic reporting platform. real users with real stakes make you a better designer and a better thinker. find the work that matters and do it carefully.

---

**take the W sometimes.**

dropping a class isn't failure. it's information. i've withdrawn from things that weren't right for the moment. the transcript shows a W. what it doesn't show is that i made a good call.

---

**your breadth is not a liability.**

silk road music. japanese language. latino film. public health. interaction design. people will try to tell you to pick a lane. your job is to build something that could only exist at the intersection of all of them.

---

**mentorship goes both ways.**

when someone invests in you, invest in someone else. not as a transaction. as a continuation.

---

**bloom where you are.**

盛放于所在之处.

even when it's unfamiliar. especially then.
