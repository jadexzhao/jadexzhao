Helping people choose and set up software

In plain English: when a company is deciding whether to buy a piece of software, someone technical needs to sit with them, answer real questions, and show that it works. That’s my lane. I join those conversations, build a working demo so people can see the thing in action, connect it to their other tools, help the sales and engineering sides understand each other, and make sure the handoff is clean when a trial turns into the real thing. The industry name for this is go-to-market (GTM) or Solutions Engineering.

It’s the same skill set the guides below describe: being technical enough to be trusted, while sitting next to the business conversation. My NYC startup internship (not yet public) is where I practice it.
